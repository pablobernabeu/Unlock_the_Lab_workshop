Your Guide to Reading Science Like a Scientist

Headlines often promise miracle cures and terrifying threats, but how much of it is backed by real science?

In this interactive workshop, you'll step into the shoes of a scientist. You'll evaluate realistic (fictional) research papers using a scientific rating system.

How It Works:

  1. Read the glossary and rating rubric to understand quality criteria
  2. For each paper:
    • Rate the study based on your scientific assessment (1-7 scale)
    • Predict what you think the average rating will be from all participants
  3. Earn points based on how accurately you predict the crowd's assessment

Your goal: Rate each study objectively AND predict how others will rate it. Can you think like both a scientist and understand the crowd?

Key Takeaways:

  • Access matters: Open science makes research accessible to everyone
  • Headlines lie: Clickbait often misrepresents actual findings
  • Methods matter: Good science requires rigorous methodology and open data
  • Context is key: Strong conclusions admit limitations and build on prior work

Keep Exploring Science!

When reading science news:

  • Look for the original research paper
  • Check if data is openly available
  • Question sensational claims
  • Consider the source and methodology
